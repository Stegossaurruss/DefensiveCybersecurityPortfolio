## Teach your family about cybersecurity topic of your choice

### Teaching my dad about RSA key-pair encryption

##### Overview
- I made a brief, simple powerpoint presentation on asymmetric encryption to teach my dad
- I chose this topic because he's quite technologically inclined and has a fair existing knowledge of basic cybersecurity concepts.
- He's also pretty good at math, so understanding moduli is not too difficult

##### Question
- He asked a question about just how long a 1024bit number is in digits, for a real world comparison, and we found out that its 309 digits.
- With some more research, we found out that with RSA-1024, there are around 2.8*10^147 primes that could be chosen for p and q.
- This is measureably more than atoms in the observable universe, so the chance of picking the same prime is mathematically impossible.
- We also found that in order to store all primes 1024bits and under, it would require around 50000 trillion times more computer storage than currently exists on Earth
