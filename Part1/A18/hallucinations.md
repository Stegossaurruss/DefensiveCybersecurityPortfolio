## Discover two hallucination cases when using a generative AI system

##### Fake legal case citations
- Through researching prominent AI hallucination cases, I found a U.S. court case (Mata v. Avianca, 2023)
- A lawyer used ChatGPT to generate legal arguments and it produced completely fake case citations that did not exist.
- The AI generated plausible but entirely false information, and when confronted to verify the validity of the cases, ChatGPT confidently insisted that the cases existed, when in fact they did not.
- A clear hallucination case in which ChatGPT confidently provided the user with false, made-up information.
- Link: (https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-65735769)


##### Google bard incorrect factual information
- Upon the debut of Google's Bard in early 2023, an LLM chatbot, it hallucinated information about the James Webb Telescope.
- It claimed that the telescope took the first ever image of a planet outside of our solar system.
- This is a false claim, as the first photos of an exoplanet were taken in 2004, 14 years before the telescope launched, as evidenced on NASA's website.
- This is a significant hallucination case because it was the first demo of Google's - a reputable tech company - chatbot model, and immediately encountered errors with the AI making up information.
- Link: (https://www.theverge.com/2023/2/8/23590864/google-ai-chatbot-bard-mistake-error-exoplanet-demo)
- Link: (https://science.nasa.gov/exoplanets/)
