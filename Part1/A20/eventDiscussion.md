## Participate in a discussion with your friends about cybersecurity events

#### Our discussion
- We held a discussion in the Week 6 Lab to further our cybersecurity knowledge and fulfill the requirements for this task.
- Here are a number of topics we discussed, mostly focused around the rise of AI and it's relevance in cyberspace.

#### Emerging cyber threats with AI
###### Prompt Injection
- Most of the members of the discussion had done some research on prompt injection for task A22, and as a result we'd learnt about the prominence of prompt injection threats to large language models.
- One member metioned that prompt injection attacks are the numer one risk in the OWASP for LLMs and prompt injection vulnerabilities are present in over half of controlled AI systems.
- While not all of us had success in performing a prompt injection thus far, we all agreed that we'd encountered similar problems with AI, like in Lab Quiz 1 in which the PDF providing had prompts inject to mislead the user and make the challenge more difficult without strategic use of the LLM.
###### Social Engineering
- Another point of discussion was the interaction of AI with social engineering, and a few good points were raised.
- Tools like voice replicators and tone replicators can be used to very effectively fool victims into being exploited.
- As AI systems are used to enhance security, like AI inbox filtering with Gmail, users may become more complacent, less aware, and as a result possibly be more susceptible to phishing or other similar scams.
- On top of this, the ease of use and volume capabilities of LLMs make it significantly easier to create large scale, convincing attacks from a social engineering perspective, lowering the skill, knowledge and effort required for successful malicious activities.
######  AI data collection
- Another point of concern raised was with the way the LLMs gather data on users. Usage over time provides the model with a surprisingly large amount of personal information, especially if the user isn't careful to restrict what it tells the model.
- The group raised concerns over how that information is stored, what it is used for, and possibly whether or not that data, and all user data is being sold to third-party data buyers.
- We weren't sure exactly about the policies involved, legally and with individual LLMs, but with our general distrust for large companies we all agreed that we should learn more about it.
