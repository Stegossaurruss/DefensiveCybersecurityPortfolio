## Discover security concepts used in a public space

##### Parking meter (combined with parking checkers)
- This parking meter is on the west side of UWA, just out the front of the Computer Science building.
- Parking meters provide access control to parking spots, requirement a payment depending on a set of rules set by the parking space.
- If the payment is not provided, in this case a UWA parking officer will issue a fine to the offending car.
- This puts financial incentive to follow the access control rules set by the University for the parking.

##### Traffic cameras
- Traffic cameras are usually paired with traffic lights, and are used to enforce the following of the rules set by the traffic lights.
- If a driver runs a red light or stops too far into the intersection, this traffic camera will record that breach as evidence to issue a fine to the driver.
- It is very dangerous to disobey traffic lights and it is important to the security of all drivers on the road that everyone is following the same rules to ensure safety, and traffic cameras help to enforce this.

##### Tag-accessed drop gate
- This is the drop gate used to access the parking lot for the sailing club I am a member of, and use to park and then walk to UWA.
- I have a small blue tag that I press against the reader, which causes the gate to drop to the ground and I can drive over it.
- This is an example of "something I have" access control to a restricted parking lot reserved for members only.
