## Discover 3 local security incidents

##### Legal Practice Board of Western Australia
- Through some research about cybersecurity incidents in Perth, I discovered the the LPBWA was attacked and breached in mid 2025.
- The Direwolf ransomware group accessed some corporate data and some personal information, but I was not able to find technical specfications due to a legal injunction I believe.
- Direwolf ransomed the data and compromised the security of the LPMWA, who are now vigilant for misuse of that data.
- Link: (https://www.lpbwa.org.au/cyber-incident)
- Link: (https://www.cyberdaily.au/security/12158-exclusive-legal-practice-board-of-western-australia-confirms-dire-wolf-ransomware-attack)

  
##### UWA cyberattack
- UWA faced a cyberattack in mid 2025, with the university detecting unauthorised access to university password information.
- As a result of this breach, all staff and students were locked out of all UWA systems and were required to change their passwords.
- It was not disclosed how many students' passwords were accessed, but UWA immediately began enacting counter-measures upon attack.
- I discovered this attack because of the communciations to students last semester. (Also had to change my password.)
- Link: (https://www.cybersecurityintelligence.com/blog/university-of-western-australia-forced-into-online-lockdown-8638.html)


##### Harry Perkins Institute
- A ransomware group known as Medusa successfully accessed and stole a large amount of data, around 4.6 terabytes.
- This data largely consisted of security camera footage throughout the facility as well as some other information.
- A ransom of $700000 was posted and then ignored, and the attackers claimed to have posted the data online.
- I discovered this with more research of cybersecurity incidents in Perth, a clear breach of confidentiality.
- Link: (https://www.watoday.com.au/national/western-australia/perth-medical-research-facility-internal-camera-footage-published-online-hackers-claim-20240716-p5ju9f.html)
